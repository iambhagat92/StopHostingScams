Is the "Price Lock" Too Good To Be True?

Most hosting companies are like drug dealers. The first hit is cheap (or free), but once you're hooked, the price skyrockets. InterServer claims to be different with their "Price Lock Guarantee."

What It Actually Means

We read their Terms of Service so you don't have to. Here is the breakdown:

  • Sign-up Price: The price you pay when you create your account.
  • Renewal Price: The price you pay next month or next year.
  • The Guarantee: InterServer promises that the standard rate of the plan you purchased will NOT change for the life of your account.
Important Note: If you use a Black Friday coupon to get hosting for $0.01 for the first month, your price will revert to the current standard rate ($2.50 or $7.00 depending on the offer) after that month. But once you are on that standard rate, it locks.

Compared to Competitors

Let's look at 5-year projected costs:

  • InterServer: $2.50/mo x 60 months = $150
  • Bluehost: $2.95/mo (first year) + $11.99/mo (4 years) = $610
  • HostGator: $3.75/mo (first year) + $11.95/mo (4 years) = $618

The difference is staggering. You literally save over $450 just by choosing the honest company.

Verdict

The Price Lock Guarantee is legit. It is the main reason we rank InterServer #1 for 2026. They don't penalize loyalty.

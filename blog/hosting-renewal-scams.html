The "Renewal Price" Scam: How Hosts Triple Your Bill

ED

Editorial Team

Updated: Feb 12, 2026

It happens every day. You sign up for hosting at $2.95/month. You are happy. Three years later, you get an automated email receipt for $142.80. You panic. Welcome to the "Renewal Trap."

How The Scam Works

Large hosting conglomerates (like Newfold Digital, which owns Bluehost and HostGator) operate on a "loss leader" strategy. They lose money on your first year to get you locked into their ecosystem.

Once your website is built and you are comfortable, your initial term expires. That's when the Regular Rate kicks in.

The Math is Scary:

  • Bluehost Intro: $2.95/mo
  • Bluehost Renewal: $11.99/mo
  • Increase: 406%

Why InterServer is Different

InterServer uses a different business model called the Price Lock Guarantee. It is simple: the price you sign up with is the price you keep.

  • If you sign up at $2.50/mo, you renew at $7.00/mo (standard rate).
  • BUT, if you use a coupon to get it for $2.50, the max it ever goes to is the standard $7.00.
  • Unlike competitors hitting $15-$20, InterServer stays affordable.

3 Ways to Avoid the Trap

  1. Read the TOS: Look for "Renewal Rates" in the footer of the pricing page.
  2. Avoid 36-Month Contracts: If you have to pay for 3 years to get a deal, you are being trapped.
  3. Choose Monthly Hosting: Providers like InterServer allow month-to-month payments so you can leave anytime.

Conclusion

Don't fall for the big marketing budgets. Check the renewal price first. If it's hidden, run away.

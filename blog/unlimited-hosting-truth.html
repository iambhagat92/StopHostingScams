The "Unlimited" Hosting Lie: What They Hiding?

By StopHostingScams Team • Feb 15, 2026 • 10 Min Read

You see it on almost every hosting website: "Unlimited Storage! Unlimited Bandwidth! Unlimited Websites!" all for the price of a cup of coffee.

It sounds amazing. Who wouldn't want infinite resources?

But ask yourself this: If your hard drive at home gets full, you have to buy a new one. Hard drives cost money. Electricity costs money. Servers cost money. So how can a company give you "unlimited" anything for $2.95 a month?

The answer is simple: They don't. It's a marketing myth. And if you don't understand the fine print, your website could disappear overnight.

1. The "File Count" Trap (Inodes)

This is the most common way "unlimited" hosts shut you down. While they claim to give you unlimited space (GB), they secretly limit the number of files you can have. This is called an Inode Limit.

Every file on your server—every email, every image, every system folder—counts as one Inode. Many budget hosts set a limit of 100,000 to 200,000 inodes.

It sounds like a lot, but a typical WordPress installation with a few plugins and a year's worth of blog posts can easily hit 150,000 files.

"We recently had a reader whose site was suspended by HostGator because he had 'too many files', even though he was using less than 1GB of his 'Unlimited' space."

How InterServer is different:

InterServer gives you unlimited storage AND a much higher inode ceiling. They are known for being extremely lenient with file counts compared to Bluehost or GoDaddy.

2. The "Fair Use" Policy

Buried deep in the Terms of Service of every "Unlimited" host is a clause called the "Fair Use Policy" or "Resource Usage Policy."

It basically says: "You have unlimited resources... unless you use too many resources."

If your website gets a sudden spike in traffic (which should be a good thing!), or if a plugin starts using a bit too much CPU power, the host will "throttle" your site.

What does throttling look like?

  • Your website takes 10 seconds to load.
  • Visitors see a "508 Resource Limit Reached" error.
  • You get an email forcing you to upgrade to a VPS plan or be terminated.

3. Unlimited Bandwidth vs. Speed

"Unlimited Bandwidth" just means they won't charge you for the amount of data transferred. It does NOT mean that data will transfer fast.

Think of it like an all-you-can-eat buffet. Yes, the food is unlimited, but there is only one line, and the service is slow. If 1,000 people show up at once, nobody eats.

Cheap hosts cram 2,000+ customers onto a single server. Even if you have "unlimited" bandwidth, you are fighting with 1,999 other people for the server's CPU and RAM.

4. The "Unlimited Websites" Bait

Many plans offer "Unlimited Websites" on their mid-tier plans. This is useful if you want to host multiple small projects.

However, remember that all your websites share the same resources. If you have 5 sites on one Shared Hosting account, and one of them gets hacked or goes viral, ALL 5 sites will go down.

So, What Should You Do?

Don't fall for the word "Unlimited." Instead, look for hosts that are transparent about what you actually get.
